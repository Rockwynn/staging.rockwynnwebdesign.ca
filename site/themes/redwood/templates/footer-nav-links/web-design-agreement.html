Getting down to the nitty gritty

Design

I will create look-and-feel designs, and flexible layouts that adapt to the capabilities of many devices and screen sizes. I use Responsive Layout templates developed from HTML5 markup and CSS2.1 + 3 Style sheets for styling. I create designs iteratively so I won’t waste time mocking up every template as a static visual. You’ll have plenty of opportunities to review the work and provide feedback. I’ll share the Staging Site with you and have regular, possibly daily contact. If, at any time, you’re not happy with the direction our work is taking, you’ll pay me in full for everything I have produced until that point and cancel this contract.

I know from experience that fixed-price contracts are rarely beneficial to you, as they often limit you to your earliest ideas. I don’t want to limit your ability to change your mind. The fixed price at the beginning of this contract is based on the number of pages and the length of time I estimate I’ll need to accomplish everything you’ve told me you want to achieve, but I’m happy to be flexible. If you do want to change your mind, add extra pages or templates or even add new functionality, that won’t be a problem as I’ll provide a separate estimate for that. Along the way I’ll ask you to put your requests in writing so I can keep track of changes.

Page Content

You are responsible for supplying all page content. Text content must be submitted in electronic format (.txt, .rtf, .doc) via google docs or email attachment, no faxes, scanned, or handwritten pages accepted. You wouldn’t want to pay designer rates for steno work!! Graphic files should be submitted in an editable, vector digital format, whenever possible. Photos should be in a high resolution digital format. If you’d like me to write new content, search for images or take photos for you, I can provide a separate estimate for that.

Browser testing

Browser testing no longer means attempting to make a Website look the same in browsers of different capabilities or on devices with different size screens. It does mean ensuring that a person’s experience of a design should be appropriate to the capabilities of a browser or device.

Desktop browser testing

I test the work in current versions of major desktop browsers including those made by Apple (Safari), Google (Chrome), Microsoft (Internet Explorer) and Firefox. We’ll also test to ensure Microsoft Internet Explorer 9 for Windows users get an appropriate, possibly different, experience. I won’t be testing in old or abandoned browsers unless you specify otherwise.

Mobile browser testing

Testing popular small-screen devices is essential in ensuring that a person’s experience of a design is appropriate to the capabilities of the device they’re using. I test the work in: iOS: Safari, Google Chrome Android 4.x: Google Chrome and Firefox. I currently don’t test Blackberry, Opera Mini/Mobile, or other mobile browsers.

Search Engine Registration

I will optimize your project with appropriate titles, keywords and descriptions and after your Website launch, submit your project to Google Search Engine.
